\begin{abstract}
%<Context>
The rapid digitalization of services in the recent years has brought many challenges for software companies. While amount of the collected data increases rapidly, in many cases firms are lacking the resources and the expertise to process the data at hand. Depending on the portfolio of the business the nature of the data might differ, nevertheless it often includes demographic data gathered about users and data generated during the usage of the software. Conducting careful analysis on user data should be an important aspect in the daily activities of software companies, because it may reveal previously unseen trends and hidden patterns, which strongly contributes to the enhancement of the overall service package. 

%<Objectives>
An objective of this thesis is to study how user data is seen in related studies and what techniques are utilized for its analysis. As the concept of user data is not commonly used among researchers, its scope and understanding for the purposes of this study is introduced. A more practical objective of this thesis is to apply Data Mining techniques for audience engagement purposes in the Choicely voting platform based on the collected data. 

%<Methods>
This study uses Exploratory Data Analysis to get a high-level overview on the data at Choicely. Computer Vision is put into use in order to enhance the image content additional data and prepare the vote transactions for Association Analysis. The Apriori algorithm is used to extract frequent itemsets, whose lift values are studied carefully in the latter part of the study. Finally, Co-Clustering is utilized to identify the underlying structural patterns of votes for demographic groups as well as content of the participants' images. The analysis is focused more on a single contest hosted in the platform, but some insights on the system-level analysis are shared as well. 

%<Results>
Results show that beauty, sport and entertainment contests have a long history and success in the platform. The results have pinpointed many flaws and biases in the data. For example, many users have not filled the demographic information on their profiles which puts limitations to the findings. The chosen techniques however demonstrate their capabilities in extracting patterns from the data. The results offer the potential of analyzing differences between genders and age groups in terms of engagement. The developed methods can also suggest what kind of content tends to be more engaging to which demographic groups of users. The evaluation on the statistical significance on the findings is challenging due to the nature of the platform, however it would be interesting direction for reserachers of the future.

\end{abstract}
